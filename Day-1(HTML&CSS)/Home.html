My Hometown Dharmavaram

Dharmavaram is located at 14°26′N 77°43′E (or 14.43°N, 77.72°E) and has an average elevation of 345 meters (1131 feet).

Demographics

As per the 2006 India Census, Dharmavaram had a population of approximately 150,400. Males constitute 51% of the population and females 49%. The average literacy rate is 56%, which is lower than the national average of 59.5%. Male literacy stands at 65%, while female literacy is 46%. About 13% of the population is under 6 years of age.

Economy and Culture

Dharmavaram is renowned as a major hub for silk production. The town has a dedicated area for weaving and selling silk garments, and the silk produced here is considered among the most famous in India.

Transport

The Dharmavaram railway station is a junction, and nearly all trains stop here for at least five minutes, making it a key transit point.

Geography

The town also features the Chitravati Lake, which adds to its natural landscape..
